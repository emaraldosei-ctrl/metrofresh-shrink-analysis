## Project Overview

This Power BI project investigates inventory shrinkage and inventory record accuracy (IRA) across MetroFresh Convenience Stores.

The analysis was designed to identify where shrink occurs, understand why it occurs, evaluate inventory accuracy, and provide actionable recommendations to reduce operational losses and improve profitability.

The project combines inventory data, adjustment records, cycle counts, product information, and store performance metrics to deliver executive-level and operational insights.

---

## Business Problem

MetroFresh Convenience Stores was experiencing inventory shrinkage above industry benchmarks, resulting in significant financial losses and operational inefficiencies.

Key challenges included:

* High inventory shrink rates
* Poor visibility into shrink root causes
* Store-level performance variation
* Category-specific loss concentration
* Inventory record accuracy concerns
* Limited operational visibility into loss drivers

---

## Project Objectives

### 1. Identify Where Shrink Occurs

Determine which stores, store formats, and product categories generate the highest losses.

### 2. Identify Why Shrink Occurs

Analyze loss attribution across:

* Expiry
* Damage
* Theft
* Employee consumption
* Other inventory adjustments

### 3. Analyze Trends Over Time

Evaluate monthly and daily shrink patterns to identify unusual spikes and recurring trends.

### 4. Measure Inventory Accuracy

Assess Inventory Record Accuracy (IRA) against operational targets.

### 5. Evaluate Store Performance

Identify stores contributing disproportionately to overall shrink.

### 6. Generate Actionable Recommendations

Provide evidence-based recommendations to reduce inventory loss and improve operational efficiency.

# Key Insights

### Shrink Rate Exceeds Target

The overall shrink rate of 2.44% exceeds the operational target and industry benchmark, indicating a significant loss prevention opportunity.

### Expiry Is the Largest Loss Driver

Expiry-related inventory adjustments account for the highest proportion of recorded losses, highlighting weaknesses in stock rotation and inventory management practices.

### Urban Stores Drive Most Shrink

Urban-format stores account for the largest share of shrink losses, suggesting additional monitoring and controls may be required.

### Category-Level Risk Concentration

Beer, Wine, Vape, Cigarettes, and General Merchandise consistently exceed shrink targets and represent the highest-risk categories.

### Loss Concentration Among Stores

A small number of stores contribute disproportionately to total shrink value, indicating localized operational issues.

### Strong Inventory Accuracy

Inventory Record Accuracy (IRA) exceeds target performance levels, providing confidence in the reliability of reported inventory data.

# Recommendations

## High Priority

### Improve Expiry Management

* Strengthen FIFO processes
* Introduce markdown triggers before expiry
* Improve replenishment planning

### Focus on High-Loss Stores

* Conduct targeted audits
* Increase cycle counting frequency
* Monitor operational compliance

### Strengthen Urban Store Controls

* Improve CCTV coverage
* Increase inventory checks
* Enhance loss prevention procedures

---

## Medium Priority

### Improve Shrink Attribution

* Reduce "Other" adjustment categories
* Improve reason-code discipline
* Enhance root-cause visibility

### Investigate Shrink Spikes

* Review unusual daily and monthly shrink events
* Implement automated anomaly detection

---

## Low Priority

### Sustain Inventory Accuracy

* Continue cycle-count programs
* Maintain inventory governance standards
* Monitor IRA performance regularly
